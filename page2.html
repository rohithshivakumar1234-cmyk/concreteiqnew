IS 10262:2019 Key Formulas

Target Mean Strength
f'ck = fck + 1.65 × S
fck = characteristic strength, S = standard deviation (IS 10262 Table 1)
Water-Cement Ratio (Abrams' Law)
f'ck = A / B^(w/c)
A = 98 MPa, B = 3.5 (OPC 53), empirical constants
Water Content (IS 10262 Table 2)
W = Wref ± ΔW (slump/zone)
Adjust ±3% per 25mm slump change
Aggregate Volume
V = 1 - (C/ρc + W + SP/ρa) / 1000
V = total aggregate volume, ρc = cement density = 3150 kg/m³
Split Tensile Strength (IS 456:2000)
ft = 0.7 √fck MPa
Empirical relation for normal weight concrete
Modulus of Elasticity (IS 456)
Ec = 5000 √fck MPa
Short-term static modulus for design purposes

How to Use ConcreteIQ

A quick guide to all features — works on iOS, Android and Desktop

1
Login to the Platform
Select your role (Student / Professor / Site Engineer / RMC Plant), enter your name, then type one of the demo codes: ADMIN, GUIDE, TEAM1, or TEAM2. Tap Access Platform.
2
AI Aggregate Detector — Camera
Go to AI Detector from the top menu or Dashboard. Tap the camera area — your browser will ask for camera permission, tap Allow. Point the camera at an aggregate sample and tap 📸 Capture Image. Captured images appear below the camera.
3
AI Aggregate Detector — Upload
Tap 📁 Upload to select one or more images from your gallery or file system. You can upload multiple images at once. Only upload real aggregate photos (crushed stone, gravel, coarse aggregate) — not screenshots or documents.
4
Analyse Images with AI
After capturing or uploading images, tap 🔍 Analyse All. The AI will examine each image and classify it:

✓ AGG — Valid aggregate (accepted)

✕ REJ — Not an aggregate or poor quality (rejected)

The Analysis Summary panel on the right shows totals, acceptance rate and size breakdown.
5
Mix Design Calculator
Go to Mix Design in the top menu. Select your concrete grade (M20–M100), exposure, slump, aggregate size and cement type. Tap ⚗️ Calculate Mix Design to get IS 10262:2019 compliant proportions instantly.
6
Strength Prediction & Reports
After Mix Design and AI Detector analysis, go to Strength Prediction. It uses ResNet50 + LSBoost to predict compressive strength at 7, 14 and 28 days — both from mix inputs and from your captured aggregate images. Compare results, view charts and read AI suggestions. Then use Reports to generate PDF/DOCX.
📱 iOS / iPhone Tips
• Open this page in Safari (not Chrome) on iPhone for best camera support
• When camera permission is requested, tap Allow
• If camera doesn't start: iPhone Settings → Safari → Camera → Allow
• To upload photos: tap 📁 Upload → choose from your photo library
• Scroll horizontally in tabs if menu items appear cut off
🌐 How to Publish ConcreteIQ Online
Option 1 — Netlify (Recommended, Free, 60 seconds)
1. Open app.netlify.com in your browser
2. Sign up free with GitHub or email
3. Drag and drop the ConcreteIQ_v5.html file
4. Your site is live at: https://random-name.netlify.app
5. Rename under Site Settings → Custom Domain
Option 2 — GitHub Pages (Free, Version Controlled)
1. Create free account at github.com
2. Create a new repository: concreteiq
3. Upload ConcreteIQ_v5.html, rename to index.html
4. Settings → Pages → Branch: main → Save
5. URL: https://yourusername.github.io/concreteiq
Option 3 — Vercel (Free, Fast CDN)
1. Open vercel.com → Sign up free
2. Import from GitHub or drag & drop
3. Rename file to index.html before uploading
4. Live at: https://yourproject.vercel.app
⚠️ Before Publishing — Change These
• Replace access codes (ADMIN, GUIDE, TEAM1, TEAM2) with your own secure codes
• Update robots meta tag: change noindex,nofollow to index,follow if you want search visibility
• Test camera on real mobile before sharing the link
• Use HTTPS hosting — camera API requires HTTPS (all options above use HTTPS by default)
